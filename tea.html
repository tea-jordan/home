Liu Bao Tea 六堡茶

Liu Bao tea is a fermented tea produced in China's Guangxi province. It is a staple product in Hong Kong, as well as in Malaysia. Some sources say that Liu Bao tea was exported to Malaysia due to its popularity among tin mine workers, who drank the tea to aid their health in response to the harsh mine conditions. For this reason there is a lot of Liu Bao tea stored in Malaysia, which has a warm and humid environment that help move the tea's post-fermentation / aging process along. The tea tastes woody, earthy, and has a faint aromatic pine needle smell. In my experience Liu Bao teas tend to taste cleaner than ripe pu-er teas, which sometimes come out a bit murky from fermentation. Very warming, and a delicious tea to drink in the winter!
